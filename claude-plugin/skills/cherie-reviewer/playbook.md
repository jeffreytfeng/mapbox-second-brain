# Cherie Review Playbook

*Source: internal Mapbox prep doc on preparing for Cherie reviews. Captured 2026-05-04. Mapbox-internal use only.*

A document that can answer the four gates below is well-prepared for executive scrutiny by Cherie Wong (CTO). Each gate has the question, what it tests, and Cherie's actual phrasing for failures — used to predict pushback.

---

## Gate 1 — Strategy Clarity & Goal Definition

**Test:** Does the plan have a specific, measurable "finish line"? Vague goals are a major red flag.

- **Clinical not marketing.** Replace "achieve victory with BMW" with "replace HERE with our SDK for BMW by Q2 2026." Cherie pushes for the specific finish line and the "mile marker for 2026."
- **Connect the work to revenue.** Every initiative must trace cleanly to the revenue target.

**Likely pushback if it fails:**
> "It's great if we can get to 89 million... I don't know how to connect the dots and how you got there... none of it adds up."

---

## Gate 2 — Believable Financial Story

**Test:** Are the numbers defensible and grounded in a credible pipeline?

- **Justify growth with a pipeline.** Named customer opportunities, potential ARR per opportunity, confidence rates. A goal without a pipeline is a hope.
- **Scrutinize the business case.** Investment must match return. Disproportionate asks get called out.

**Likely pushback if it fails:**
> "You all just need a pipeline... if your win rate has been 50%. Then you need a 32 million pipeline at least."
> "Crazy pants" — said about a business case requiring a large team for small returns.

---

## Gate 3 — Realistic Execution Plan

**Test:** How will the team actually do the work? Move beyond slogans into specifics.

- **Concrete roadmap, not slogans.** No "fluff" / "bumper stickers." Each major goal needs an execution plan with milestones.
- **Pre-aligned dependencies.** Cross-team buy-in must exist BEFORE the review, not after. One-sided dependencies are a major risk.

**Likely pushback if it fails:**
> "Very fluffy... like you've thrown bumper stickers in there and then there's no real road map behind it."
> "You should have actually gotten alignment... It can't be an after OP conversation."

---

## Gate 4 — Resource Justification

**Test:** Is every dollar and every person accounted for? Efficiency and operational leverage are non-negotiable.

- **AI mandate (NON-NEGOTIABLE).** Every incremental headcount request must first explain how AI tools were tried/leveraged to avoid the hire.
- **Justify HC with data on what's broken.** Explain what is failing in the current model that necessitates the increase.

**Likely pushback if it fails:**
> "I would not approve any incremental funding." — said when AI mandate isn't addressed.
> "What is not working about the the swings today or the sacrifices that you're making that necessitate all of that."

## Appendix — Additional Pointers

### Strategic Clarity & The "Why"
- **Frame technical work in business outcomes.** A security upgrade is "compliance to retain OEM partners," not "security upgrade."
- **Justify strategic shifts.** If the plan reverses last year's strategy, state explicitly what changed.

### Financial Rigor & Business Viability
- **Reconcile against last year's OP1.** A track record of past initiatives generating revenue builds confidence.

### Operational Reality & Execution Plan
- **Phased milestones.** Year-long goals broken into H1 deliverables or quarter-by-quarter.
- **Plan for volatility.** Account for resource thrashing and unplanned reactive work. Plans assuming perfect execution are unrealistic.
- **Clinical assessment.** Major decisions (e.g., replacing a vendor) need objective, data-driven analysis.

### Cross-Functional Alignment
- **Single named accountable owner** per cross-functional initiative. "Handshakes" don't count.
- **Resolve blockers ahead of review** — known cross-org under-resourcing should be addressed, not discovered.

### Resource Justification & Efficiency
- **Platform hires as force multipliers.** Quantify how a central-team hire frees up multiple engineers across the org.

### Document Structure & Storytelling
- **Prioritize hairy topics.** More document space on the most ambiguous, ambitious, important items — guide the conversation toward them.
- **Celebrate wins.** Brief section on past-year successes builds credibility.
- **Honest performance assessment** vs. last year's goals. Shows learning, builds trust.
