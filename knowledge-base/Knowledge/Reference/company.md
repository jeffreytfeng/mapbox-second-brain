# Mapbox - Company Overview

## Company Facts

- **Founded:** 2010 (Washington, DC / San Francisco, CA)
- **Stage:** Series C ($280M raised in 2021, led by SoftBank Vision Fund 2)
- **Employees:** ~900 (as of 2026)
- **Valuation:** ~$1B+ (unicorn, private)
- **Revenue:** Private (not disclosed)

## Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Give developers the tools to build the world's best location experiences.

**Vision:** The standard platform for location — maps, navigation, and search — built on developer-first principles.

**Core belief:** The default mapping solutions (Google Maps) are black boxes. Developers and enterprises deserve a fully customizable, high-performance location platform they can own.

## Company History

- **2010:** Founded by Eric Gundersen and team; emerged from open-source mapping community
- **2015:** Series A — focus on developer SDKs and map customization
- **2017:** Series B — expanded into navigation and mobility use cases
- **2019:** Series C initial close — major push into enterprise and autonomous vehicle market
- **2021:** $280M Series C extension (SoftBank) — scale global platform
- **2023–2024:** Profitability push; strategic restructuring; focus on core developer and enterprise segments
- **2025–2026:** Platform consolidation; AI-powered location features; renewed enterprise growth

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**Google Maps Platform**
- Strengths: Ubiquity, data quality, brand recognition
- Weaknesses: Lock-in, no customization, high cost at scale, opaque
- Our angle: Full customization, better developer experience, no lock-in

**HERE Technologies**
- Strengths: Enterprise relationships, automotive heritage, HD maps
- Weaknesses: Legacy stack, poor developer experience, slow iteration
- Our angle: Modern APIs, better SDKs, stronger community

**Esri / ArcGIS**
- Strengths: Deep GIS market penetration, government/enterprise
- Weaknesses: Enterprise sales-heavy, expensive, developer-hostile
- Our angle: Developer-first, cloud-native, faster to ship

**AWS Location Service**
- Strengths: AWS ecosystem integration, competitive pricing
- Weaknesses: Limited customization, behind on features
- Our angle: Richer feature set, better rendering performance

### Emerging Competitors

- **Felt** — Collaborative map editing (niche)
- **Protomaps** — Open-source self-hosted tiles
- **TomTom** — Automotive expanding to developer platform

## Company Culture

**Values:**
1. **Open by default** — Rooted in open-source; share knowledge, data, and tools
2. **Developer empathy** — Build what developers actually need, not what sounds impressive
3. **Ownership** — Small teams with full accountability end-to-end
4. **Move fast with quality** — Speed matters, but so does trust (location data is safety-critical)

**Communication norms:**
- Distributed team across US, Europe
- Async-first culture; deep docs for decisions
- Hybrid with SF and DC offices as anchors

## Key Risks (Internal)

1. **Google dependency:** Many customers default-evaluate Google Maps first; sales cycle requires justifying switch
2. **Pricing sensitivity:** Enterprise deals are large and lumpy; logo churn is visible
3. **Data freshness:** Map data quality is a constant investment; falling behind on POI/road data hurts trust
4. **AI disruption:** LLM-based navigation and search could shift what "location" means for developers
